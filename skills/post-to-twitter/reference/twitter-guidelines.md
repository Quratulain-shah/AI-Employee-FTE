## Twitter Platform Essentials

### Character Limits
- **Single Tweet:** 280 characters max
- **Optimal Length:** 71-100 characters (highest engagement)
- **Links:** Auto-shortened to 23 characters (t.co)
- **Username Mentions:** Count toward 280
- **Media:** Images/GIFs/polls don't count toward limit

### Audience Characteristics
- **Demographics:** Tech-savvy, 25-45, professional
- **Behavior:** Fast scrolling, quick engagement, real-time
- **Mindset:** News, trends, conversations, hot takes
- **Attention Span:** 2-3 seconds to hook

## Tweet Best Practices

### Optimal Tweet Length
**Sweet Spot: 71-100 characters**
- Gets 17% more engagement than longer tweets
- Quick to read, easy to retweet
- Mobile-friendly (most users on mobile)

**Under 71 characters:** Too short, may seem low-effort
**100-280 characters:** Fine, but loses some engagement
**Over 200 characters:** Lowest engagement (thread instead)

### Hashtag Strategy (Critical Difference)
**Twitter: 1-2 hashtags maximum**
- Unlike Instagram (5-10), Twitter users avoid hashtag-heavy tweets
- Hashtags don't significantly boost reach on Twitter
- Focus on content quality > hashtag quantity

**When to Use Hashtags:**
- Trending topics (#AI #TechNews)
- Event hashtags (#SXSW #TechConf2026)
- Branded hashtag (#BuildInPublic #YourBrand)

**When to Skip Hashtags:**
- Hot takes (looks more authentic without)
- Replies/conversations
- Threads (use only on first/last tweet)

## Thread Structure

**When to Use Threads:**
- Topic requires > 280 characters meaningfully
- Step-by-step tutorials
- Storytelling with narrative
- In-depth analysis
- Listicles (5+ items)

**Optimal Thread Length:** 5-7 tweets
**Maximum Recommended:** 12 tweets (longer = fewer readers)

### Thread Opening (Tweet 1)

**Must Include:**
1. Hook (first 5 words critical)
2. Promise of value (what they'll learn)
3. Thread indicator (üßµ or "A thread:" or "Let me explain üëá")

**Example:**
```

Actual automation, not just tips.

A thread üßµ
```

### Thread Body (Tweets 2-N)

**Format:**
- Number clearly: "1/" "2/" "3/" etc.
- One point per tweet
- 150-250 characters per tweet (sweet spot)
- Natural flow between tweets

**Example Tweet 2:**
```
1/ Email Auto-Responder

Set up Claude to read incoming emails, draft contextual replies, and queue them for your approval.

Went from 2 hours/day on email to 15 minutes.
```

### Thread Conclusion (Final Tweet)

**Must Include:**
1. Summary or key takeaway
2. CTA (follow, retweet, comment)
3. Optional: Link to resource

## Writing Style

### Voice & Tone
- **Conversational:** Like talking to a colleague
- **Direct:** No fluff, get to the point
- **Authentic:** Show personality, not corporate speak
- **Confident:** Have an opinion, but not arrogant

### Formatting Tips

**Use Line Breaks:**
```
This is hard to read because it's all one block and Twitter users scroll fast so they'll skip this.
```

vs.

```
This is easier to read.

Line breaks help.

Short sentences work.
```

**Sentence Fragments Are OK:**
```
Automation? Game changer.
Productivity? 10x improvement.
Worth it? Absolutely.
```

**Avoid:**
- All caps (looks aggressive)
- Excessive punctuation (!!!)
- Corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, circle back)
- Long words when short ones work

## Engagement Tactics

### Get More Replies
- Ask direct questions
- End with "What's your experience?"
- Invite disagreement (respectfully)
- Reply to every comment in first hour

### Get More Retweets
- Make it quotable (71-100 char sweet spot)
- Include valuable insight
- Be controversial (but not offensive)
- Clear formatting (easy to screenshot)

### Get More Likes
- Relatable content
- Humor (if it fits your brand)
- Validating opinions
- Beautiful formatting

## Timing & Frequency

### Best Posting Times
**Weekdays:**
- 8:00-10:00 AM (morning routine)
- 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch break)
- 5:00-6:00 PM (commute/evening)

**Weekends:**
- 9:00-11:00 AM (lazy morning scrolling)

**Avoid:**
- Late night (after 10 PM)
- Very early morning (before 7 AM)
- Weekday afternoons (3-5 PM lowest engagement)

### Posting Frequency
- **Minimum:** 3-5 tweets per week
- **Optimal:** 1-2 tweets per day
- **Maximum:** 5-7 tweets per day
- **Threads:** 1-2 per week max

**Spacing:** 4-6 hours between tweets minimum

## Twitter Algorithm Factors

**What Boosts Reach:**
1. Early engagement (first 15 min)
2. Retweets with comments
3. Replies (especially threads of replies)
4. Profile clicks
5. Link clicks
6. Media (images, videos, GIFs)

**What Hurts Reach:**
- Low engagement rate
- Links to certain external sites
- Controversial content (depends on network)
- Mutes/blocks after seeing tweet

**Pro Tip:** Twitter shows your tweets first to your most engaged followers. If they engage, reach expands.

## Content Mix Recommendation

**Weekly Balance:**
- 40% Value/Insights (tips, learnings)
- 30% Engagement (questions, discussions)
- 20% Personal/Behind-the-Scenes
- 10% Promotional (services, offers)

**Thread Distribution:**
- 1 thread per week (max 2)
- Threads for tutorials, stories, deep dives
- Single tweets for everything else

**Key Metrics:**
1. **Impressions:** How many times tweet was seen
2. **Engagement Rate:** (Engagements / Impressions) √ó 100
3. **Link Clicks:** If tweet has link
4. **Retweets:** Shares (most valuable)
5. **Replies:** Conversation depth
6. **Profile Visits:** How many checked your profile

**Benchmarks:**
- Engagement Rate: 1-3% average, 5%+ excellent
- Retweets: 5-10% of total engagements is strong
- Replies: 20%+ of engagements = good conversation starter
