## Content Length Guidelines

### Optimal Length: 150-300 Words

**Why this range?**
- Short enough to read in 60-90 seconds
- Long enough to provide real value
- Maximizes engagement on LinkedIn algorithm
- Avoids "See more" truncation on mobile (1300 characters â‰ˆ 200 words)

### Length by Post Type

| Post Type | Ideal Length | Character Count |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Quick Tip/Insight** | 100-150 words | 650-975 chars |
| **Standard Post** | 150-250 words | 975-1625 chars |
| **Story/Case Study** | 200-300 words | 1300-1950 chars |
| **Long-form Thought Leadership** | 250-400 words | 1625-2600 chars (rare) |

### Structure by Length

**Short Post (100-150 words):**
```
[Hook - 1 sentence]

[Main point - 2-3 sentences]

[Takeaway/CTA - 1 sentence]

#hashtags
```

**Standard Post (150-250 words):**
```
[Hook - 1-2 sentences]

[Context - 2-3 sentences]

[Main content - 3-5 sentences with bullets if needed]

[Takeaway/CTA - 1-2 sentences]

#hashtags
```

**Long Post (250-300 words):**
```
[Hook - 1-2 sentences]

[Story/Context - 3-4 sentences]

[Main points - 5-7 sentences, use bullets/numbering]

[Lesson learned - 2-3 sentences]

[CTA - 1-2 sentences]

#hashtags
```

## Topics to Avoid

### âŒ Prohibited Topics

**1. Politics & Controversial Issues**
- Political opinions or endorsements
- Partisan debates
- Controversial social issues
- Religious discussions

**Why:** Alienates potential clients, damages professional brand

**2. Negative Competitor Commentary**
- Criticizing other businesses
- Naming competitors negatively
- "We're better than [X]" comparisons
- Industry gossip

**Why:** Unprofessional, reflects poorly on you

**3. Client Confidentiality Breaches**
- Client names without explicit permission
- Proprietary methodologies
- Financial details
- Sensitive business information

**Why:** Legal and ethical violations, trust erosion

**4. Complaints & Negativity**
- Venting about difficult clients
- Complaining about industry problems without solutions
- Burnout/overwhelm posts (exception: if framed as lesson learned)
- "Woe is me" content

**Why:** Unprofessional, repels potential clients

**5. Sales-Heavy Pitches**
- Hard selling or aggressive promotion
- "Buy now" pressure
- Discount announcements (LinkedIn isn't a store)
- Constant self-promotion without value

**Why:** LinkedIn algorithm penalizes, audience unfollows

**6. Personal Oversharing**
- Family drama or personal problems
- Health issues (unless directly relevant to professional journey)
- Financial struggles
- Relationship issues

**Why:** Inappropriate for professional network

## Call-to-Action Patterns

### CTA Purpose: Engagement vs. Conversion

**Engagement CTAs (Use 80% of the time):**
- Goal: Start conversation, build relationships
- Examples:
  - "What's your experience with [topic]?"
  - "How do you handle [situation]?"
  - "Agree or disagree?"
  - "What am I missing?"
  - "Drop a comment if [X] resonates"

**Conversion CTAs (Use 20% of the time):**
- Goal: Generate leads, drive business
- Examples:
  - "If this sounds familiar, let's talk."
  - "Curious if this could work for you? Send me a message."
  - "Dealing with [problem]? Happy to share what worked."

### CTA Formulas

**1. Question CTA (Most Common)**
```
[Insight/story]

What's your approach to [topic]?
```

**2. Invitation CTA**
```
[Value provided]

If you're facing [problem], let's talk about solutions.
```

**3. Offer to Help CTA**
```
[Lesson shared]

Happy to share more details if this is useful. Drop a comment or DM.
```

**4. Validation CTA**
```
[Opinion/observation]

Am I the only one who thinks this? Let me know your take.
```

**5. Story Continuation CTA**
```
[Part 1 of story]

Want to know what happened next? Part 2 coming Thursday.
```

### CTA Best Practices

- **Placement:** Always end with CTA (last 1-2 sentences before hashtags)
- **Tone:** Conversational, not pushy
- **Specificity:** Ask specific questions (not "What do you think?")
- **Authenticity:** Genuinely interested in responses
- **Variety:** Rotate CTA types (don't always ask questions)

### Examples: Weak vs. Strong CTAs

âŒ **Weak CTAs:**
- "Thoughts?" (too vague)
- "Let me know what you think" (generic)
- "Follow for more content" (self-serving)
- "Like if you agree" (engagement bait)

âœ… **Strong CTAs:**
- "How do you balance automation with human oversight in your workflow?"
- "Ever tried this approach? What worked (or didn't) for you?"
- "If inbox overwhelm sounds familiar, let's talk about solutions that actually work."
- "What's the one task you wish you could automate tomorrow?"

## Quality Checklist

### Pre-Publish Checklist (Apply to Every Post)

#### Content Quality
- [ ] **Hook:** First sentence grabs attention
- [ ] **Value:** Post provides useful insight, tip, or story
- [ ] **Clarity:** Main point is clear and easy to understand
- [ ] **Relevance:** Content aligns with audience interests (business, tech, productivity)
- [ ] **Authenticity:** Voice sounds genuine (not corporate speak)

#### Structure & Format
- [ ] **Length:** 150-300 words (optimal engagement range)
- [ ] **Line Breaks:** Every 2-3 sentences for readability
- [ ] **Bullets/Numbers:** Used where appropriate (lists, results)
- [ ] **CTA:** Ends with clear call-to-action or question
- [ ] **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant tags (not excessive)

#### Brand Voice
- [ ] **Professional:** Maintains credibility and authority
- [ ] **Approachable:** Conversational without being unprofessional
- [ ] **Helpful:** Focuses on providing value, not selling
- [ ] **Authentic:** Sounds like a human, not a press release

#### Technical Quality
- [ ] **Spelling:** Zero typos
- [ ] **Grammar:** Correct sentence structure
- [ ] **Punctuation:** Proper use throughout
- [ ] **Capitalization:** Consistent and correct

#### Safety & Compliance
- [ ] **No Confidential Info:** No client names without permission
- [ ] **No Prohibited Topics:** Politics, negativity, competitor attacks avoided
- [ ] **Professional Standards:** Appropriate for public, permanent content
- [ ] **Factual Accuracy:** Claims backed by real experience/data

#### Engagement Optimization
- [ ] **Visual Breaks:** Emojis or bullets for scannability
- [ ] **Mobile-Friendly:** Reads well on small screens
- [ ] **Question/CTA:** Invites comments or engagement
- [ ] **Timing Consideration:** Posting during optimal hours (noted for scheduling)

## Content Score: Rate Your Post

### Scoring System (Out of 10 points)

**8-10 points:** Excellent, publish immediately
**6-7 points:** Good, minor tweaks needed
**4-5 points:** Needs revision
**0-3 points:** Start over

#### Evaluation Criteria

1. **Hook Strength** (2 points)
   - 2: Compelling, makes you want to read more
   - 1: Decent but not gripping
   - 0: Generic or boring opening

2. **Value Provided** (2 points)
   - 2: Actionable insight, clear takeaway
   - 1: Somewhat useful
   - 0: No real value or learning

3. **Voice Authenticity** (2 points)
   - 2: Sounds genuinely you, personable
   - 1: Professional but a bit generic
   - 0: Corporate speak, robotic

4. **Structure & Readability** (2 points)
   - 2: Easy to scan, well-formatted
   - 1: Readable but could be better
   - 0: Wall of text, hard to read

5. **CTA Effectiveness** (2 points)
   - 2: Clear, engaging invitation to interact
   - 1: Present but weak
   - 0: No CTA or generic "Thoughts?"

### Example Scoring

**Example Post:**
```
Just automated 20 hours/week for a client. [Hook: 1/2 - specific but short]

They were drowning in email. 200+ daily, 30% needing responses, 2 hours every morning sorting. [Context]

Built an AI assistant with automated categorization, response drafting, and human approval workflow. [Value: 2/2 - clear solution]

Result: Email time dropped from 2 hours to 20 minutes daily. [Metric]

Inbox overwhelm is solvable. [Voice: 2/2 - authentic, confident]

What's the task you wish you could automate tomorrow? [CTA: 2/2 - specific question]

#EmailAutomation #Productivity #AIforBusiness
```

**Score: 9/10** - Excellent post, ready to publish

## Monthly Content Review

**Schedule:** First Monday of each month

**Review Questions:**
1. Which posts got the most engagement? Why?
2. Which topics resonated with audience?
3. What CTAs drove the most comments/connections?
4. Any negative feedback or controversial reactions?

**Adjustments:**
- Update templates based on what works
- Refine voice if needed
- Rotate topics if one category dominates
- Test new CTA approaches
