# Content Calendar Template

**Version:** 1.0 | **Updated:** 2026-01-11

---

## Purpose

A structured content calendar ensures consistent posting, balanced content mix, and strategic planning across Facebook and Instagram. This document provides templates, themes, and scheduling guidelines.

---

## Weekly Posting Schedule

### Recommended Frequency

**Facebook:**
- **Minimum:** 3 posts per week
- **Optimal:** 4-5 posts per week
- **Maximum:** 7 posts per week (daily)

**Instagram:**
- **Minimum:** 4 posts per week
- **Optimal:** 5-7 posts per week (daily)
- **Maximum:** 2 posts per day (morning + evening)

**Stories (Both Platforms):**
- Daily or every-other-day
- 3-5 story frames per session

## Weekly Theme Structure

### Monday: Motivation / Week Kickoff
**Facebook:**
- Type: Motivational quote or mindset post
- Length: 50-70 words
- Goal: Start week with inspiration

**Instagram:**
- Type: Quote graphic or workspace shot
- Caption: 150-250 words of storytelling
- Goal: Aspirational content to set tone

**Example Topics:**
- "How we approach Mondays differently"
- "Weekly goal setting ritual"
- "Mindset shift that changed everything"
- "Monday motivation for solopreneurs"

---

### Tuesday: Value / Educational
**Facebook:**
- Type: Quick tip or insight
- Length: 60-80 words
- Goal: Provide actionable advice

**Instagram:**
- Type: Carousel tutorial (5-7 slides)
- Caption: 200-300 words explaining value
- Goal: Position as expert, save-worthy content

**Example Topics:**
- "3 automation hacks for busy founders"
- "How to structure your day for deep work"
- "The tool that saved us 10 hours this week"
- "Productivity tip: Time blocking method"

---

### Wednesday: Behind-the-Scenes / Process
**Facebook:**
- Type: Community question or discussion starter
- Length: 40-60 words + question
- Goal: Spark engagement and conversation

**Instagram:**
- Type: Behind-the-scenes photo or video
- Caption: 150-200 words showing process
- Goal: Humanize brand, authenticity

**Example Topics:**
- "What our workspace actually looks like"
- "The messy middle of [project type]"
- "How we handle [common challenge]"
- "A day in the life of [your role]"

---

### Thursday: Portfolio / Showcase
**Facebook:**
- Type: Project milestone or result
- Length: 70-90 words with story
- Goal: Demonstrate capabilities

**Instagram:**
- Type: Before/after or portfolio carousel
- Caption: 250-350 words with project story
- Goal: Showcase expertise, attract clients

**Example Topics:**
- "Project walkthrough: Concept to launch"
- "Client success story and results"
- "Before/after transformation"
- "This week's completed projects"

---

### Friday: Engagement / Community
**Facebook:**
- Type: "Weekend question" or poll
- Length: 30-50 words + engaging question
- Goal: Build community, high comment rate

**Instagram:**
- Type: Relatable content or user engagement
- Caption: 100-150 words + CTA
- Goal: Fun, light content before weekend

**Example Topics:**
- "What are your weekend productivity goals?"
- "Fill in the blank: This week I accomplished ___"
- "Tag a fellow entrepreneur who inspires you"
- "Friday wins: What did you accomplish this week?"

---

### Saturday: Optional - Light Content
**Facebook:**
- Type: Weekend update or casual share
- Length: 40-60 words
- Goal: Stay present without heavy content

**Instagram:**
- Type: Lifestyle or personal content
- Caption: Short (100-150 words)
- Goal: Show human side, relatability

**Example Topics:**
- "How we recharge on weekends"
- "Weekend reading recommendations"
- "Saturday productivity (or rest) mode"
- Optional: Skip posting, use Stories instead

---

### Sunday: Rest or Planning Content
**Facebook:**
- **Best Practice:** No posting (lowest engagement day)
- **Alternative:** Schedule Monday post prep

**Instagram:**
- Type: Planning/reflection post (optional)
- Caption: Weekly recap or week-ahead preview
- Goal: Accountability and transparency

**Example Topics:**
- "Week in review: Wins and lessons"
- "Next week's focus and goals"
- "Sunday reset ritual"
- Optional: Skip posting, use Stories for casual updates

## Content Batching Strategy

### Weekly Batching Workflow

**Option 1: Weekly Batch (Recommended)**
- **When:** Friday afternoon or Sunday evening
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Output:** 7-10 posts drafted for next week
- **Benefit:** Consistent quality, reduced daily decision fatigue

**Option 2: Bi-Weekly Batch**
- **When:** Every other Sunday
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours
- **Output:** 14-20 posts drafted for next 2 weeks
- **Benefit:** Longer planning horizon, better strategic alignment

### Batching Process Steps

1. **Content Audit (15 min)**
   - Review Business_Goals.md
   - Check recent completed projects in /Done
   - Identify content-worthy moments from past week

2. **Platform Planning (20 min)**
   - Assign content to Facebook vs Instagram
   - Select content types per day (follow weekly themes)
   - Identify image requirements

3. **Drafting (90 min)**
   - Write all captions in one sitting
   - Apply platform-specific guidelines
   - Add hashtags (Instagram)
   - Create approval requests

4. **Visual Prep (30 min)**
   - Identify/select images for each post
   - Run through image optimizer
   - Organize in /Social_Media/[Platform]/Ready/

5. **Approval Queue (10 min)**
   - Create approval requests for all posts
   - Schedule approval deadlines
   - Notify yourself to review

## Emergency Content Ideas

When you need content fast, use these evergreen templates:

### Quick Facebook Posts (5-min prep)
1. **Quick Tip:** "Today's productivity tip: [one actionable tip]"
2. **Question:** "Quick question: How do you handle [common challenge]?"
3. **Recommendation:** "Tool we're loving this week: [tool name and why]"
4. **Gratitude:** "Grateful for [specific thing]. What are you grateful for today?"

### Quick Instagram Posts (10-min prep)
1. **Quote + Your Take:** Design quote → add your perspective in caption
2. **Workspace Shot:** Photo of desk → caption about what you're working on
3. **Screenshot + Story:** Share tool/app screenshot → explain how you use it
4. **Repost + Credit:** Share someone else's post (with permission) → your commentary

## Content Approval Workflow Integration

### Before Batching
1. Check `/Business_Goals.md` for current priorities
2. Review `/Company_Handbook.md` for content guidelines
3. Note any restricted topics or required approvals

### During Batching
1. Draft all content following templates
2. Apply platform-specific guidelines
3. Select images and optimize

### After Batching
1. Create approval requests via `handle-approval` skill
2. Save to `/Pending_Approval/` with clear deadlines
3. Set reminders to review before publish dates

### Human Review
1. Review batched content 2-3 days before post date
2. Edit as needed directly in approval files
3. Move approved posts to `/Approved/`
4. System auto-publishes at scheduled times

## Tips for Consistency

### Build Momentum
- Start with 3x/week, increase gradually
- Don't skip weeks (algorithm penalties)
- Stories keep you visible between posts

### Repurpose Content
- Turn one blog post into 5 social posts
- Carousel Instagram → Individual Facebook posts
- Long LinkedIn article → Thread on Twitter → Summary on FB/IG

### Track What Works
- Double down on high-performing content types
- Repeat successful formulas with new topics
- Don't abandon formats too quickly (test 3-5 times)
